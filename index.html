Please, this is not the place to say restructured water or garlic or bitter melons prevent cancer.

Please do not redirect question or re-edit title. I want this hard hitting and succinct: we very much need a structured approach to preventing cancer which will in coming years affect one half of the word's population if nothing is done!

I have read a large number of answers about specific things like garlic and vitamins preventing cancer, but they are just that. It is my belief that while cancer is a disease of genetics and that there are many, many, different types of cancer, that cancer prevention should have a structured approach.

I read one answer that flatly stated cancer cannot be prevented and others that hinted at the same thing. I very much disagree. It is this attitude that has dissuaded building prevention investigations in both funding and in research.

But there are powerful forces opposing prevention programs. For example, witness the efforts of the tobacco companies from the mid-fifties to the present day. Tobacco is a well researched mutagen which causes cancer. Yet, tobacco usage has leveled off in developed countries and continues to grow in lesser developed regions.

So how do we put together a comprehensive cancer prevention program?

Want to know how to reduce your overall risk of cancer?

In Layman’s terms, Cancer cells are made of amino acids and protein. However, they are glucose dependent and can only burn sugar. In some exceptions, most types of cancer and tumours are only able to burn through blood sugar (glucose) as a fuel source to maintain their metabolism.

However, If you are able to starve them, the cancer cells would break down their amino acids (proteins) for fuel to maintain existence. This gives the body a stronger chance to fight them as the cancer cells shrink due to starvation of the cells. This process is similar towards muscle tissue; if your muscle tissues are starving, they break their own protein down into glucose.

Optimising the duration and survival rate of cancer correlates with 2 important factors.

1) Insulin sensitivity
2) Nutrient partitioning

Cancer cells have the highest rate of insulin sensitivity of any tissue the human body can make. This means cancer cells have a higher need for insulin, therefore they draw more insulin, glucose and sugar to drive and grow their existence. Cancer cells are now competing for resources amongst other cells in the body. Insulin is your body’s primary transporter of glucose into your cells; which means they take all your blood sugar and distributes them towards various cells that need glucose. As of result, cancer cells tend to win in competition for resources with other cells, leading to more nutrition (calories, glucose, sugar) for cancer cells to grow faster.

Now muscle tissue, particularly trained muscle accumulated from weight training/resistance training tends to have a higher insulin sensitivity.

Understand that…
Weight lifting = bigger muscles, which makes them more sensitive to insulin at all times, even when the body is resting!

As a result, the body needs to produce less insulin to transport the same amount of glucose towards your muscle tissue. This is because if you produce too much insulin, your blood sugar levels drop faster, leading to hypoglycemia. Therefore, your body ends up producing less insulin.

Less circulating insulin = Cancer cells and tumours get less fuel (calories)

All forms of exercise whilst actively utilising the body pull in glucose and insulin for fuel/energy. However, you are not exercising 24 hours a day. Low intensity cardio (LISS) only improves insulin sensitivity of the duration of the session, (e.g. 1 hour of walking) therefore it is not optimal for generating a continuous cycle of improved insulin sensitivity. This leaves the remaining 23 hours for the cancer cells to attempt to grow again due to the reduction of optimised insulin sensitivity.

On the other hand, weight training leaves a 24 hour effect on the body due to the daily need of fuel for muscle tissue to maintain and grow. When continuously weight training over time; this improves insulin sensitivity and overtime you are able to accumulate more muscle tissue; which leads to muscle tissue competing for insulin against cancer cells. Furthermore, you are creating a higher level of insulin sensitivity due to increased muscle mass, leading to a higher demand for glucose. By gaining muscle, you essentially create more insulin sensitive tissue, which allows the muscle to directly compete with the growing cancer cells. Everyone is predisposed towards getting cancer unfortunately; however the body aims to dispose of it before they start to grow. If not, this is when cancer cells and tumours start to grow. Additionally, resistance training can drop risks of cancer by 40%! This means if you had a 5% of developing (X) type of cancer, it would drop down to 3%. Furthermore, people undergoing cancer treatment who continue training are able to almost DOUBLE their survival rate and beating the cancer.

Overall, any form of resistance training is highly beneficial towards our daily lifestyles. This can include high intensity training due to large amounts of glucose needed to perform these activities. From this, the body becomes more insulin sensitive and needs glucose to recover and repair. I hope this was informative and let me know what you think of this post!
